About Me

Dr. Diksha Goel is a Research Scientist at CSIRO’s Data61 in Melbourne, Australia, specializing in Cybersecurity, Artificial Intelligence, Game Theory, and Graph Data Mining. Her research focuses on developing intelligent, AI-powered security solutions to combat evolving cyber threats and protect critical infrastructures. She earned her Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Adelaide, where she explored the intersection of AI and Cybersecurity, designing advanced frameworks for adaptive threat detection, cyber resilience, and autonomous defense strategies. Her expertise spans machine learning-driven security, game-theoretic defense modeling, and large-scale graph analysis for vulnerability detection. Before transitioning to her role as a Research Scientist, Dr. Goel was a Postdoctoral Fellow at CSIRO’s Data61, where she contributed to national cyber defense efforts through the Cyber Security Cooperative Research Centre (CSCRC). She has published extensively in leading journals and top-tier cybersecurity conferences, with her work influencing both academic advancements and industry practices. Dr. Goel is passionate about bridging theoretical research with real-world applications to develop scalable, proactive, and intelligent security frameworks. Committed to innovation and excellence, she strives to strengthen digital infrastructures, enhance cyber resilience, and create a safer, AI-driven cyber landscape.

Research

My research is at the cutting edge of cybersecurity, focusing on AI-driven solutions to protect critical infrastructures. The key areas of my work include:

  • Intelligent Security Solutions: Developing advanced defensive frameworks using reinforcement learning, machine learning, lifelong learning, and evolutionary computation for effective threat detection, rapid response, and mitigation.

  • Graph-Based and Game-Theoretic Cybersecurity: Developing graph neural network-based models to analyze network structures and identify critical vulnerabilities. Additionally, formulating game-theoretic frameworks to simulate adversarial scenarios and optimize defense strategies against cyber threats.

  • Data-Driven and Evolutionary Approaches: Utilizing data mining techniques and evolutionary algorithms to build scalable, proactive defenses that adapt to evolving cyber threats.
